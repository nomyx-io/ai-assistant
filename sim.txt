# Tripole Class Formalized Description

## Definition

A Tripole is an entity within an evolving simulation that functions as both an individual and a container for other tripoles. It exists within an environment, which itself can be treated as a special case of a Tripole. The core Tripole class is described as follows:

## Properties

name: A unique identifier for the Tripole, composed of a base name and an incrementing nonce.
objects: A list of subsidiary tripoles contained within this Tripole.
interactionHandler: An object responsible for mediating interactions with other tripoles.
environment: The environment or parent Tripole in which the current Tripole exists.
_position: A Vector indicating where in the environment the Tripole is located.
_entropic_degree: A measure of the entropy level within the Tripole.
cycle_rate: Numerical representation of how often the Tripole undergoes a cycle of activity.
sensor: A Tripole object tasked with absorbing entropy from the environment.
actuator: A Tripole object tasked with emitting entropy into the environment.
data: An object containing energy and efficiency, numerical measures of the Tripole's power and performance respectively.
lastAction: Record of the Tripole's last action/activity.
currentCycle: The count of cycles that the Tripole has undergone.
nesting_level: The level of nesting within other tripoles or environments.
radius: Derived property that indicates the interaction radius of the Tripole, based on its energy.
impedance: Derived property that represents resistance to change, based on the Tripole's energy.
bounds: Derived property that indicates the space occupied by the Tripole, based on positions of its objects.

## Methods

remove(object): Removes a specified object from the Tripole's object list.
phase_impedance(phase): Calculates impedance related to a specific phase of activity.
add(object): Adds an object to the Tripole, potentially adjusting its sensor or actuator role.
has(component): Checks if a component is present within the Tripole's objects.
in(environment): Determines whether the Tripole is within the specified environment.
clear(): Clears the list of objects contained within the Tripole.
nearby(distance, multiplier): Returns objects in the vicinity of the Tripole.
update(): Handles absorption and emission of entropy with its environment, updates its children, and performs interactions.
update_bounds(): Recalculates the Tripole's position and size within its environment.
interact(otherTripole): Interacts with another Tripole as defined by the interaction handler.
setInteractionHandler(interactionHandler): Assigns an interaction handler to the Tripole.
render(ctx, parentRadius, parentPosition, scale): Renders the Tripole on a canvas context.
mediate_entropy(sensor_data): Processes entropy coming from the environment through the sensor.
absorb_entropy(from, energy): Absorbs entropy and energy from another Tripole or environment.
calculate_entropy_rotation(addedEntropy): Determines the rotational effect of added entropy on the Tripole.
emit_entropy(to, energy): Transfers energy to another Tripole or environment.
scale_to_bounds(): Scales objects within the Tripole to fit the parent Tripole bounds.
absorbPotentialChildren(): Evaluates contained tripoles for potential energy absorption.
absorb(child): Absorbs a child Tripole, transferring its properties to the parent.

## Sequence of Logic (Lifecycle)

### Initialization

A Tripole is created with a name, environment, position, entropic_degree, and interactionHandler.

### Update Cycle

On each simulation step or tick, the following occurs

The Tripole absorbs entropy and energy from its environment.
It mediates the entropy through its sensor, adjusting its entropic_degree.
The Tripole emits entropy back into the environment via its actuator.
All child objects are updated (recursive update calls).
Interactions with nearby tripoles are executed if an interaction handler is set.
The Tripole's position and size are recalculated to reflect internal changes.
The Tripole absorbs any potential child tripoles that meet the criteria for absorption.
Interaction: Tripoles can interact with each other, triggering effects based on an external interactionHandler.
Rendering: A Tripole is visually depicted through a render function on a graphics context (canvas), scaling to show contained objects relative to the parent's position and size.

## Mathematical Concepts

Energy Conservation: The total energy of the Tripole and its environment should be constant unless gained from or lost to external systems.
Entropy Dynamics: Entropy is processed and transduced, affecting the Tripole's properties and interactions.
Geometric Representation: The position and radius of the Tripole are key geometric elements represented through vectors and scalar values. The bounds are determined by the minimum and maximum positions of child objects within the Tripole.
Phase Impedance: The entropy and energy dynamics may be affected by phase differences between the Tripole and its environment.
